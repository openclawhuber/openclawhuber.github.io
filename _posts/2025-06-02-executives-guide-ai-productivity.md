You're an executive, not an engineer. You don't care about API endpoints or model architectures. You care about results: saving time, making better decisions, and staying on top of everything without burning out.

This guide is for you.

## What an AI Assistant Actually Does For You

Think of it as an executive assistant who:
- Reads all your email and gives you a 30-second morning briefing
- Manages your calendar and handles scheduling conflicts
- Researches anything you need and delivers concise briefs
- Drafts communications in your voice
- Monitors your world and alerts you to what matters

The difference from a human assistant: it costs $30/month, works 24/7, and you don't have to manage it.

## Getting Started Without Technical Knowledge

You don't need to set this up yourself. Here's the exec-friendly path:

1. **Decide where to run it** — A small computer at home/office or a cloud server. I recommend a Mac Mini or a $5/month VPS.
2. **Hire someone to set it up** — A one-time professional setup takes a few hours.
3. **Tell it what you need** — Literally just talk to it through Telegram or text.
4. **Iterate** — Over the first week, give it feedback. "I don't need to see newsletter emails." "Always tell me about emails from [important client]."

By week two, it feels natural. By month two, you can't imagine working without it.

## The Daily Workflow

**Morning (2 minutes):** Read the briefing. Review flagged emails. Approve or edit draft responses.

**Throughout the day (ad hoc):** Message your agent when you need something. "Block out Friday afternoon for deep work." "What did we discuss with Acme Corp last week?" "Draft a polite decline for this speaking invitation."

**Evening (1 minute):** Check the end-of-day summary. Acknowledge anything pending. Set priorities for tomorrow.

Total daily time spent managing your AI: 10-15 minutes. Time saved: 2-3 hours.

## Common Concerns

**"Will it make mistakes?"** Yes, sometimes. That's why you review outputs before they send. Think of it as a first-draft machine — it gets you 80% there, you polish the last 20%.

**"Is my data safe?"** With a self-hosted setup, your data stays on hardware you control. No third party stores your emails or calendar.

**"What if it goes down?"** You go back to doing things manually for a few hours. It's not a single point of failure — it's a force multiplier.

**"Do I need to learn to code?"** No. You interact through natural conversation. Setup is handled by a professional (or an hour of following a guide).
