It's late 2025 and AI agents have matured rapidly. Here's an honest assessment of what works, what doesn't, and what's coming.

## What Works Well

**Email management** — AI triage is solved. Categorization, summarization, and draft generation work reliably. Most users report 50-70% time savings on email.

**Scheduling** — Calendar integration and natural language scheduling are mature. Conflict resolution, timezone handling, and smart suggestions all work.

**Research** — Web search, synthesis, and summarization are strong. The AI can compile research briefs that used to take interns hours.

**Communication drafting** — Writing emails, messages, and updates in your voice is surprisingly good after a brief training period.

**Proactive monitoring** — Scheduled checks, alerts, and daily briefings work reliably.

## What Mostly Works

**Browser automation** — Functional but brittle. Website changes break automations. Good for periodic tasks, not for mission-critical workflows.

**Multi-step workflows** — Chaining multiple tools together works but sometimes fails partway through. Needs human oversight for important tasks.

**Voice interaction** — TTS is excellent (ElevenLabs). Speech-to-text is good. Full voice-first interaction is usable but not seamless.

**Memory and personalization** — File-based memory works well. The agent genuinely learns your preferences over weeks. But it's not perfect — sometimes it forgets or misremembers.

## What Doesn't Work Yet

**Fully autonomous operation** — We're not at the point where you can trust an AI agent to handle everything without oversight. For important decisions, human review is still essential.

**Real-time conversation** — Phone calls, video meetings, real-time negotiation — these are still human territory.

**Creative judgment** — The AI can draft, but it can't make creative decisions that require taste, intuition, or deep domain expertise.

**Physical world interaction** — Beyond smart home basics, AI agents can't interact with the physical world meaningfully.

## The Technology Stack in 2026

The typical production setup:
- **Hardware:** Mac Mini M3/M4 or $5/month VPS
- **Platform:** OpenClaw or similar open-source agent framework
- **Models:** Claude Sonnet (default), GPT-4o (coding), Gemini (long context)
- **Channels:** Telegram + email + calendar
- **Cost:** $20-50/month total

This gets you a capable, always-on AI assistant that handles the majority of administrative work.

## What's Next

The next 12 months will likely bring:
- Better multi-agent coordination
- Improved autonomous decision-making
- More reliable browser automation
- Local model capabilities catching up to cloud
- Standard security frameworks for AI agents

We're past the hype cycle and into practical deployment. The technology works. The question is no longer "can AI agents help?" but "how quickly can I get one set up?"
