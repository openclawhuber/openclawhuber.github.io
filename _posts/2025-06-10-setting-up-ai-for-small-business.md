Enterprise companies spend millions on AI. Small businesses can get 80% of the same benefit for under $50/month. Here's how.

## What Small Businesses Need

Most small businesses need help with the same things:
- Responding to customer inquiries faster
- Managing schedules and appointments
- Following up on leads and proposals
- Organizing documents and information
- Handling repetitive communications

None of these require cutting-edge AI. They require a reliable, always-on assistant that handles the grunt work.

## The Setup

### Hardware: $0-6/month

Use an old computer you already have, or spin up a VPS for $5/month. You don't need anything fancy.

### Software: Free

OpenClaw is open source. Installation is a single command.

### AI Costs: $10-30/month

You pay per API call to the AI model. For a typical small business workload (50-100 messages per day), expect $10-30/month.

### Total: $15-36/month

That's less than one hour of a minimum-wage employee's time.

## Quick Wins for Small Businesses

**Customer Response:** Configure your AI agent to draft responses to common inquiries. "What are your hours?" "Do you offer X?" "How much does Y cost?" Instead of answering the same questions 20 times a day, you review and send pre-drafted responses.

**Appointment Scheduling:** Connect your calendar and let customers book through your AI agent. No more phone tag.

**Lead Follow-Up:** "If we get an inquiry and I haven't responded in 4 hours, send a 'Thanks for reaching out, I'll get back to you shortly' message." Never leave a potential customer hanging.

**Daily Operations:** "Send me a summary every morning: pending invoices, today's appointments, any urgent emails."

## A Real Example

I set up a small accounting firm with OpenClaw:

- Incoming client emails get categorized (tax question, document submission, billing, scheduling)
- Tax questions get a drafted response pulling from a FAQ database
- Document submissions get acknowledged automatically
- Scheduling requests get handled by the calendar integration
- The accountant starts each morning with a 3-line summary instead of digging through 40 emails

Result: 2 hours saved per day, faster client response times, nothing falls through cracks.

## Getting Started

1. Start small — pick ONE thing (usually email or scheduling)
2. Get it working well before adding more
3. Iterate based on real usage
4. Expand capabilities as you get comfortable

Don't try to automate everything on day one. Start with the highest-impact, lowest-risk automation and build from there.
