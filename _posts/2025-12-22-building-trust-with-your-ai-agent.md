You wouldn't hand the keys to your business to a stranger on day one. Your relationship with your AI agent should follow the same trust-building arc.

## The Trust Ladder

### Level 1: Observer (Week 1)
Let the agent watch. It reads your email summaries but doesn't respond. It sees your calendar but doesn't schedule. You're learning what it can see and how it interprets information.

### Level 2: Advisor (Week 2-3)
The agent suggests actions but doesn't take them. "You have a scheduling conflict on Thursday — want me to move the 2pm?" "This email looks urgent — want me to draft a response?" You approve or reject each action.

### Level 3: Assistant (Month 1-2)
You approve categories of actions. "Auto-respond to scheduling requests. Acknowledge all client emails. Summarize newsletters." The agent acts within defined boundaries, and you spot-check occasionally.

### Level 4: Operator (Month 3+)
The agent handles most routine tasks autonomously. You review a daily summary of actions taken. You intervene only on exceptions or high-stakes decisions.

## How to Build Trust Quickly

1. **Start with low-stakes tasks.** Email triage and calendar management. Mistakes here are annoying but not catastrophic.

2. **Review outputs early.** For the first week, read every draft the agent produces. You'll quickly calibrate your trust level.

3. **Give feedback.** "That response was too formal" or "Always include the project name in subject lines." The agent adapts.

4. **Monitor the logs.** Check what your agent did each day. Not because you're suspicious, but because it builds confidence in the system.

5. **Expand gradually.** Add one new capability at a time. Get comfortable before adding more.

## When Trust Breaks

It will happen. Your agent will:
- Miscategorize an important email as low priority
- Draft a response with a wrong detail
- Miss a nuance in a scheduling request
- Generate something that doesn't match your tone

This is normal. Fix it, give feedback, and move on. One mistake doesn't mean the system is broken — it means it's learning.

## The Payoff

Clients who follow this trust ladder consistently report the same thing: by month three, they can't imagine working without their agent. The investment in gradual trust-building pays off in a system you genuinely rely on.

The executives who struggle are the ones who try to go from zero to full automation in a day. That's like hiring an assistant and giving them admin access to everything on their first morning.

Trust is built, not installed.
