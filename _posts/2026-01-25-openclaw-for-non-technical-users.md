"I'm not technical" is the number one reason people give for not setting up an AI agent. Let me address this directly: you don't need to be technical to *use* OpenClaw. You might need help *setting it up*, but using it is as simple as sending a text message.

## Using OpenClaw (Zero Technical Knowledge Required)

Once it's set up, here's what your daily interaction looks like:

**Open Telegram. Send a message.** That's it.

"What's on my calendar today?"
"Draft a response to John's email about the budget"
"Remind me to call Sarah at 3pm"
"Research the best CRM for a 5-person team"
"Schedule a 30-minute call with Mike next week"

Natural language. No commands. No code. No technical anything.

## Common Concerns

**"What if it breaks?"**
Your setup person can maintain it (my maintenance plan is $50/month), or OpenClaw will often tell you what's wrong and how to fix it.

**"What if I say something wrong?"**
You can't break it by talking to it. It's conversational. If it misunderstands, just clarify.

**"Can I use it on my phone?"**
Yes — through Telegram, which works on iPhone, Android, and desktop.

**"Do I need to update it?"**
Periodically. With a maintenance plan, this is handled for you.

**"What about my privacy?"**
Your data stays on your hardware. That's the whole point of self-hosted.

## What Non-Technical Users Love Most

Based on feedback from my non-technical clients:

1. **Morning briefings** — Start every day knowing what's ahead
2. **Email summaries** — Never drown in inbox again
3. **Quick research** — Ask anything, get a concise answer
4. **Scheduling help** — "Find time to meet with X" just works
5. **Memory** — "Remember that the Anderson project deadline is March 15th" and it actually remembers

## What Non-Technical Users Struggle With

1. **Initial expectations** — It's powerful but not magic. Give it feedback in the first week.
2. **Trusting the AI** — It takes time to trust automated responses. Start by reviewing everything.
3. **Knowing what to ask** — Experiment! The more you use it, the more use cases you discover.

## The Non-Technical User's Checklist

- [ ] Choose hardware (ask your setup person for a recommendation)
- [ ] Get Telegram installed on your phone
- [ ] Schedule a setup session
- [ ] Spend 30 minutes learning the basics after setup
- [ ] Use it daily for one week before judging

That's it. The technology is sophisticated, but your experience of it should feel like texting a very smart assistant.
