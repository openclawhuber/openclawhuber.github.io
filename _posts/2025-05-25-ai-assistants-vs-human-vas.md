Should you hire a human virtual assistant or set up an AI one? Here's an honest comparison.

## Cost

**Human VA:**
- US-based: $25-75/hour
- Overseas: $5-15/hour
- Typical commitment: 10-20 hours/month = $250-$1,500/month

**AI Assistant (OpenClaw):**
- Hardware/hosting: $0-6/month
- AI API costs: $10-30/month
- Setup (one-time): $250 with professional help
- Total ongoing: **$10-36/month**

The cost difference is dramatic. A US-based VA costs 10-40x more per month than an AI assistant.

## Availability

**Human VA:** Business hours, with notice. Time off, sick days, holidays. Async communication with response delays.

**AI Assistant:** 24/7/365. Instant response. Never sleeps, never takes vacation, never has a bad day.

Winner: AI, decisively.

## Capability

**Human VA excels at:**
- Judgment calls requiring emotional intelligence
- Tasks requiring phone calls to real humans
- Creative work (event planning, gift selection)
- Physical tasks (if local)
- Handling ambiguous, never-before-seen situations

**AI Assistant excels at:**
- Information processing (email, documents, research)
- Scheduling and calendar management
- Data entry and organization
- Repetitive tasks at scale
- Multilingual communication
- Tasks at 3 AM

Winner: Depends on the task.

## Reliability

**Human VA:** Variable. Great VAs are gold; mediocre ones create more work than they save. Finding and training a good VA takes time.

**AI Assistant:** Consistent. Does exactly what it's configured to do, every time. Doesn't learn bad habits or cut corners. But also doesn't adapt intuitively — you need to program new behaviors explicitly.

Winner: Tie (different failure modes).

## Privacy

**Human VA:** Another person with access to your accounts. You need to trust them, set up proper access controls, and accept the inherent risk of human curiosity.

**AI Assistant:** Your data stays on your hardware (self-hosted). No human ever sees it. The AI model processes it via API but doesn't retain it.

Winner: AI, for self-hosted setups.

## My Recommendation

**Start with an AI assistant** for the 80% of tasks that are information processing, scheduling, and communication. It handles these better and cheaper than a human.

**Add a human VA** if you need someone to make phone calls, handle complex judgment calls, or manage physical logistics.

Many of my clients run both: AI handles the daily grind, human VA handles the 20% that requires a human touch.
