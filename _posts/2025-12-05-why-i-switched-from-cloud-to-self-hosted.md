I was a ChatGPT Plus subscriber for over a year. It was good. I used it daily for writing, research, brainstorming. Then I switched to a self-hosted OpenClaw agent, and within a week I knew I was never going back.

## What Triggered the Switch

Three things happened in the same month:

1. **OpenAI changed their terms** — Again. More data collection, less clarity about how my conversations were used.
2. **I needed email integration** — I wanted AI to manage my inbox. Sending all my email through OpenAI's servers felt wrong.
3. **I discovered OpenClaw** — An open-source platform that could do everything ChatGPT did, plus actual integrations, running on my own hardware.

## The First Week

Day 1: Installed OpenClaw on my Mac Mini. Connected Telegram. Sent my first message. Got a response from Claude Sonnet. It worked.

Day 2: Connected email. Configured triage rules. Started getting inbox summaries on Telegram. The "aha" moment.

Day 3: Connected calendar. Set up morning briefings. Configured meeting reminders. My phone was now my mission control.

Day 4-5: Fine-tuned everything. Adjusted triage categories, tweaked briefing timing, set up cron jobs for weekly reports.

Day 6-7: Started working the way I'd always wanted. Email was managed. Calendar was smart. I had time to think instead of administrate.

## What I Gained

**Integration.** ChatGPT was a separate window I had to open. OpenClaw is everywhere — Telegram, email, calendar. It's woven into my workflow.

**Proactivity.** ChatGPT waited for me. OpenClaw reaches out. Morning briefings, meeting reminders, urgent email alerts. I don't have to remember to check things.

**Privacy.** My email content, calendar, and personal data stay on my Mac Mini. Not on OpenAI's servers.

**Control.** I choose the model. I choose the behavior. I control the memory. No algorithm deciding what to remember or forget.

**Cost savings.** ChatGPT Plus was $20/month for limited GPT-4 access. OpenClaw costs me about $25/month in API usage with unlimited access to multiple models.

## What I Lost

**Convenience of image generation.** DALL-E integration in ChatGPT is seamless. For image generation, I still use ChatGPT occasionally.

**Mobile app polish.** ChatGPT's mobile app is slick. Telegram is functional but not as polished for AI interaction.

**Zero maintenance.** ChatGPT just works. OpenClaw requires occasional updates and monitoring.

## The Verdict

For casual AI use — questions, brainstorming, one-off tasks — ChatGPT is still great. For a dedicated assistant that manages your digital life, self-hosted is the clear winner.

The setup cost is a few hours (or $250 if you hire someone). The ongoing benefit is measured in hours saved per week and peace of mind about your data.
